# Warehouse Safety Incident Reporting Procedure

**Company:** Summit Logistics | **Doc ID:** WHSE-SAF-019 | **Version:** 1.8 | **Effective:** 2026-02-28 | **Owner:** EHS Department

## Purpose & scope

Defines immediate actions and reporting requirements for Summit Logistics warehouse associates and team leads following injuries, near-misses, equipment damage, and hazardous material spills at all distribution centers.

## Key terms

- **Near-miss:** Event that could have caused injury but did not — must be reported within 24 hours.
- **First report of injury (FROI):** HR form initiated within 24 hours of recordable injury.
- **Lockout/tagout (LOTO):** Required before any repair on powered industrial trucks or conveyor.

## Standard procedure

1. Ensure scene safe — do not move injured person unless immediate danger (fire, collapse).
2. Call site emergency line ext. 991; provide bay number, injury type, number of people involved.
3. Designate one person to meet ambulance at main dock door; clear path of pallets and forklifts.
4. Preserve scene: do not operate equipment involved until EHS photos complete except life safety.
5. Team lead completes Incident Report Form IR-WH within 2 hours; witness statements same shift.
6. For near-miss: submit Near-Miss Card in kiosk — no injury required.

## Approved phrases (radio / in-person)

> "Bay 14 medical — need first aid and supervisor to aisle J-220. One associate, possible ankle injury."

> "All forklifts hold in zone C until further notice — spill cleanup in progress."

> "I'm documenting a near-miss: almost struck by reversing truck at blind corner — mirrors needed."

> "LOTO confirmed on conveyor 7 — maintenance may begin."

## Scenarios

**Scenario A — Forklift vs. rack scrape:** No injury; rack damaged. Operator stops, tags area, notifies lead. EHS inspects rack integrity before aisle reopens.

**Scenario B — Associate slip on leaking hydraulic fluid:** Minor knee injury. First aid on site; FROI initiated; spill kit deployed; area barricaded until dry.

**Scenario C — Near-miss at pedestrian crossing:** Walker almost hit — horn not sounded. Near-miss card filed; safety stand-down scheduled next shift start.

## Escalation matrix

| Severity | Notify within |
|----------|---------------|
| First aid only | Team lead + EHS same day |
| Recordable injury | Site manager + HR within 4 hours |
| Hospitalization | Regional EHS + OSHA prep within 8 hours |
| Hazmat release | Evacuate per SDS; fire dept + EHS immediately |

## Do / Don't

**Do:** Wear hi-vis vest in dock; honk at blind corners; report equipment defects before shift use.

**Don't:** Clean blood without training kit; restart equipment to "test" after incident; discourage near-miss reporting to protect metrics.
