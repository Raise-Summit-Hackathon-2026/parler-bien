# Front Desk Patient Intake & Privacy Protocol

**Company:** Northgate Medical Group | **Doc ID:** ADM-INT-022 | **Version:** 1.4 | **Effective:** 2026-01-10 | **Owner:** Practice Administration

## Purpose & scope

Guides reception and intake coordinators at Northgate Medical Group clinics for new and returning patient check-in while protecting PHI under HIPAA-aligned office policy. Covers verification, forms, copay collection, and waiting-room privacy.

## Key terms

- **PHI:** Protected health information — name, DOB, diagnosis, insurance ID, appointment reason spoken aloud.
- **Minimum necessary:** Share only information required to complete the task.
- **Verification triad:** Full legal name, date of birth, last four of SSN or photo ID.

## Standard procedure

1. Greet patient at desk; do not ask for appointment reason in open waiting room — use check-in kiosk or lowered voice.
2. Verify identity with verification triad; match to appointment in EHR (MedFlow).
3. Confirm insurance card is current; scan front and back; note copay amount in system.
4. Hand patient HIPAA notice if first visit in calendar year; obtain signature on tablet.
5. Provide clipboard or tablet for demographic updates; offer private alcove if patient needs assistance.
6. Inform patient of estimated wait; call by first name only unless duplicate first names in queue.

## Approved phrases

> "Welcome to Northgate. I'll verify a few details to pull up your chart — may I see your photo ID?"

> "Your copay today is forty dollars — we accept card or HSA. Would you like a receipt by email?"

> "For privacy, I'll ask about your visit reason when we step to the side of the desk."

> "The physician is running about fifteen minutes behind. You're checked in — we'll call you shortly."

## Scenarios

**Scenario A — New patient, incomplete forms:** Patient arrives 10 minutes early without online forms. Coordinator verifies ID, offers tablet in alcove, flags incomplete allergy list for nurse pre-visit.

**Scenario B — Wrong DOB in system:** Names match but DOB off by one day. Coordinator does not read chart aloud; asks patient to confirm DOB quietly; updates with supervisor badge if correction needed.

**Scenario C — Family member asking for status:** Visitor asks if "Mom is still in with Dr. Patel." Coordinator confirms visitor policy; does not confirm patient presence; offers to relay message through clinical staff.

## Escalation matrix

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Patient refuses ID | Supervisor; no service without verification except emergency |
| Suspected identity theft | Supervisor + compliance officer |
| Aggressive behavior in lobby | Security protocol LOB-003; other patients relocated |
| Medical emergency in waiting room | Call code team; do not move patient unless trained |

## Do / Don't

**Do:** Position monitor away from public view; shred misprints immediately; use encrypted fax for records requests.

**Don't:** Discuss diagnoses at front desk; leave sign-in sheets visible; confirm another patient's appointment to a third party without written authorization.
