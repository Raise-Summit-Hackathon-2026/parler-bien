# In-Home Care Visit Protocol & Client Dignity Standards

**Company:** CareLink Home Services | **Doc ID:** CARE-VIS-009 | **Version:** 1.6 | **Effective:** 2026-02-10 | **Owner:** Clinical Operations

## Purpose & scope

Guides CareLink Home Services care workers (CNAs, home health aides, companions) during client home visits for personal care, medication reminders, mobility assistance, and light housekeeping under the care plan.

## Key terms

- **Care plan:** Authorized tasks signed by RN supervisor — worker may not expand scope without approval.
- **Dignity-first approach:** Client chooses order of care when safe; closed doors for bathing and dressing.
- **Mandated reporter:** Worker must report suspected abuse or neglect to CareLink compliance within 24 hours.

## Standard procedure

1. Arrive within scheduled window; knock, announce name and agency; wait 60 seconds before key use per client preference on file.
2. Wash hands at entry; review whiteboard or visit checklist with client aloud.
3. Confirm identity if new client: name, DOB, address match care plan packet.
4. Perform tasks in agreed order; pause if client fatigued or in pain — document and notify RN.
5. Medication reminders only — do not administer unless MAR signed for worker level.
6. Depart: summarize completed tasks; note concerns in mobile app before leaving driveway; lock door per client instruction.

## Approved phrases

> "Good morning, Mr. Rivera — it's Ana from CareLink. Is now still a good time to help with shower and breakfast?"

> "I'll wait outside the bathroom and stay within hearing distance — call if you need me."

> "Your daughter asked us to remind you about the eleven o'clock pill — would you like water with that?"

> "I'm noting that your knee was sore today — the nurse will call this afternoon to adjust the plan."

## Scenarios

**Scenario A — Client refuses shower:** Worker offers alternative (sponge bath, reschedule); does not force; documents refusal; RN notified same day.

**Scenario B — Unsafe home (clutter, no heat):** Worker completes essential tasks only; files safety concern SC-002; supervisor visit scheduled within 48 hours.

**Scenario C — Family member directs worker to skip documented task:** Worker politely cites care plan; suggests family call RN to authorize change; does not agree off-plan.

## Escalation matrix

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Fall with injury | 911 if needed; RN + supervisor immediately |
| Suspected neglect by family | Compliance hotline; do not confront family on scene |
| Client confusion / new dementia signs | RN assessment within 24 hours |
| Worker feeling unsafe | Leave if threatened; supervisor + police per policy |

## Do / Don't

**Do:** Introduce yourself every visit if memory impaired; respect cultural preferences on same-gender care when requested.

**Don't:** Post visit details on social media; accept gifts over $25; borrow client phone or money; perform tasks not on care plan.
