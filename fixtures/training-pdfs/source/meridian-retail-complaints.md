# Customer Complaint Handling — Store Floor Standard

**Company:** Meridian Retail Co. | **Doc ID:** RET-CX-008 | **Version:** 3.0 | **Effective:** 2026-02-15 | **Owner:** Customer Experience

## Purpose & scope

All floor associates and shift leads at Meridian Retail locations must follow this standard when a customer expresses dissatisfaction about product quality, pricing, wait time, or staff interaction. Applies in-aisle, at checkout, and at the service desk.

## Key terms

- **LEARN model:** Listen, Empathize, Apologize, Resolve, Notify (manager if needed).
- **Service recovery:** Action that fixes the immediate problem (exchange, refund per policy, rain check).
- **Moment of truth:** First 30 seconds of the complaint — sets tone for entire interaction.

## Standard procedure

1. Stop what you are doing; face the customer; remove earbuds or radio chatter.
2. Listen without interrupting until the customer finishes their first statement.
3. Empathize with a specific phrase — not "I'm sorry you feel that way" alone.
4. Apologize for the experience (not necessarily admitting company fault).
5. Resolve: offer one clear next step within your authority ($25 exchange without receipt per policy).
6. Notify shift lead if customer requests manager, mentions legal action, or transaction exceeds $100.

## Approved phrases

> "Thank you for telling me — I want to make this right before you leave the store."

> "That shouldn't have happened. Let me pull up your receipt and see the fastest fix."

> "I can exchange this today or give you store credit — whichever works better for you."

> "I'm going to get my shift lead so we can authorize that refund on the spot."

## Scenarios

**Scenario A — Wrong size, no receipt:** Customer bought jeans last week, tag removed. Associate checks SKU on floor, verifies style still sold, offers exchange or store credit per 14-day policy.

**Scenario B — Long checkout line:** Customer yells that only one register is open. Associate acknowledges wait, calls for backup on radio, offers to hold items if customer needs to browse.

**Scenario C — Damaged product:** Box crushed on shelf. Associate inspects item, offers replacement from stock or 10% courtesy discount if last unit.

## Escalation matrix

| Trigger | Escalate to |
|---------|-------------|
| Request for manager | Shift lead within 3 minutes |
| Threat of social media / BBB | Shift lead + log in CX portal same day |
| Suspected fraud (duplicate receipts) | Loss prevention — do not accuse customer |
| Injury in store | Manager + incident form IR-002 |

## Do / Don't

**Do:** Lower your volume if customer is loud; use customer's name from loyalty app if available.

**Don't:** Blame another associate by name; say "That's policy" without offering an alternative; walk away mid-conversation.
