# Site Supervisor — Subcontractor Safety Induction

**Company:** BuildRight Construction | **Doc ID:** SITE-IND-005 | **Version:** 2.4 | **Effective:** 2026-01-05 | **Owner:** Safety & Compliance

## Purpose & scope

Mandatory induction checklist for BuildRight Construction site supervisors when onboarding subcontractor crews to active job sites. Covers orientation, PPE, hazard briefing, and sign-in before any work begins.

## Key terms

- **Competent person:** OSHA-defined individual who can identify hazards and has authority to stop work.
- **Toolbox talk:** 10-minute daily safety briefing documented on form TT-001.
- **Hot work permit:** Required for welding, cutting, or grinding within 35 feet of combustibles.

## Standard procedure

1. Verify subcontractor COI and signed master agreement before turnstile access.
2. Collect daily sign-in sheet with worker names, employer, trade, and emergency contact.
3. Issue site-specific orientation: laydown areas, restricted zones, restroom, muster point.
4. Confirm PPE minimum: hard hat, safety glasses, hi-vis vest, steel toes — task-specific add-ons (fall protection, respirator).
5. Walk critical hazards on site map: excavations, overhead work, crane swing radius, energized panels.
6. Subcontractor foreman signs induction form IND-005 before crew enters work zone.

## Approved phrases (supervisor to crew)

> "Before you start — stop work authority means anyone on this site can halt a task if something looks unsafe."

> "Crane lift at ten hundred — stay clear of the barricaded zone on the east elevation until all-clear on radio."

> "Your fall protection anchors are on the north parapet only — do not tie off to guardrail."

> "If you don't have a hot work permit on your belt, welding stops — no exceptions."

## Scenarios

**Scenario A — New electrical sub, live panel nearby:** Supervisor verifies qualified electrician license, establishes LOTO boundary, assigns spotter, documents in daily log.

**Scenario B — Sub arrives without hard hats:** Work stopped at gate; supervisor provides loaner PPE if available or denies entry until compliant.

**Scenario C — Near-miss reported by laborer:** Supervisor holds crew, conducts immediate toolbox talk on struck-by hazards, files near-miss before resuming.

## Escalation matrix

| Event | Notify |
|-------|--------|
| Missing COI | Project manager — no site access |
| Serious injury | 911 + PM + corporate safety within 1 hour |
| Environmental spill | Environmental consultant + stop work in zone |
| Sub non-compliance after warning | Remove from site; document in subcontractor scorecard |

## Do / Don't

**Do:** Lead by example with PPE; update hazard board when conditions change; photograph induction signatures.

**Don't:** Allow "we'll get helmets later"; skip sign-in for familiar crews; permit work during lightning within 30 miles per policy.
